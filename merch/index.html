Merch

Alohi's fully owned product line — independent of the agency, no commission owed to anyone. This is the revenue stream she builds and keeps entirely.

Why merch is different from brand deals Brand deals are income she earns by promoting someone else's product. Merch is a business she owns — she sets the prices, controls the brand, and keeps the margins. No agency cut. No approval needed.

Product Concepts

Starting ideas based on her existing audience and cosplay identity. Limited drops tied to viral moments tend to perform best.

  • Cosplay-themed apparel (hoodies, tees, crop tops)
  • Prints and art prints from popular cosplay shoots
  • Accessories (pins, keychains, phone cases) tied to specific fandoms
  • Limited edition drops around convention appearances
  • Signed memorabilia for superfans
  • Digital products: cosplay guides, preset packs, tutorials

Fulfillment Options

Print-on-demand means no upfront inventory cost — ideal for starting out. As volume grows, direct fulfillment improves margins.

  • Printify — print-on-demand, integrates with Shopify, low risk
  • Printful — similar to Printify, strong quality control
  • Shopify — storefront platform, owns the customer relationship
  • Spring (formerly Teespring) — creator-native, lower setup friction
  • Direct fulfillment — higher margin, requires inventory management

Margin Awareness

Merch is only worth pursuing if the numbers make sense. Every product needs a margin analysis before launch.

  • Product cost (printing + materials)
  • Platform fee (Shopify, Printify, etc.)
  • Shipping cost to customer
  • Payment processing fee (~3%)
  • Retail price minus all of the above = actual margin
  • Target: 40–60% margin minimum

Launch Checklist

  • Define first product or limited drop concept
  • Choose fulfillment platform
  • Set up storefront (Shopify or equivalent)
  • Run margin analysis before pricing
  • Create launch content tied to existing audience
  • Link store in TikTok and Instagram bio
  • Set up order tracking and customer email
