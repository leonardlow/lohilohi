Content Strategy

What gets posted, where, when, and why. Content creation is the top layer of the business — the part Alohi already knows. This section brings intention to it.

The 65-hour streaming requirement The agency has set a target of 65 streaming hours per month. That's roughly 15–16 hours per week — a significant commitment, especially while still employed. Understanding exactly what counts toward this number is critical before July 17th.

Platform Breakdown

  • TikTok (@lohilohi) — 1M followers, primary monetization platform. Anime, cosplay, dance, gaming fandom content.
  • TikTok LIVE — primary streaming platform for the 65-hour requirement. Gifts and subscriptions are direct income.
  • Instagram (@lohilohi) — 177K followers, strong visual presence, lower engagement rate. Supporting platform for sponsorships.
  • Other platforms — Twitch, YouTube (evaluate as she scales)

Content Pillars

The repeatable content formats that consistently perform and align with her audience.

  • Anime cosplay reveals and transformations
  • Dance videos (K-pop, anime openings)
  • Gaming fandom content (character tributes, references)
  • Cosplay skits and character portrayals
  • Behind-the-scenes of builds and production
  • Convention content and event coverage

Streaming Commitment Tracker

65 hours/month broken down to be manageable:

  • Target: ~16 hours/week (4 sessions of 4 hours)
  • Or: 5 sessions of ~3.25 hours across 5 days
  • Questions to clarify: does VOD count? Does simultaneous multistream count?
  • What platform does this need to be on? TikTok LIVE only?
  • Monthly log of actual hours streamed will be tracked here

Performance Benchmarks

Understanding what's working across platforms.

  • TikTok regular posts: 20K–90K views typical
  • TikTok strong performers: 100K–200K+ views
  • Instagram likes: 1,500–3,600 per post
  • Instagram comments: 10–20 per post (engagement gap to address)
  • Track: which content types drive brand deal inbound inquiries?
