Business Foundation

The legal and structural backbone of the business. This is what separates a hobby from a company — and what protects Alohi personally if something goes wrong.

Why this matters first Right now, Alohi is likely operating as a sole proprietor with no legal separation between her personal finances and her business. That means personal liability, no clear business credit, and tax headaches. This section fixes that.

Legal Structure

What type of business entity is this? Most creators start as sole proprietors by default — but an LLC provides liability protection and looks more credible to brands.

  • Has an LLC been formed? In what state?
  • Articles of Organization / LLC formation documents
  • Registered agent information
  • Operating agreement (even for a single-member LLC)
  • EIN (Employer Identification Number) — needed for bank account and taxes

Business Banking

All business income and expenses must flow through a separate business account. Mixing personal and business money is one of the most common mistakes young creators make — it makes taxes a nightmare and undermines the LLC protection.

  • Business checking account (separate from personal)
  • Business credit or debit card for expenses
  • PayPal or payment processor linked to business account
  • Process for moving income from TikTok/Instagram to business account

Business Identity

The professional face of the business when dealing with brands and agencies.

  • Business email address (not personal Gmail)
  • Media kit / one-pager for brand outreach
  • Standard invoice template
  • W-9 form on file (brands require this before payment)

Insurance

Often overlooked by creators — but relevant once income is real.

  • Health insurance (no longer covered by employer after July 17th)
  • Business liability insurance
  • Equipment / gear insurance for cosplay production assets
