Call Reference Sheet

Agency Fact-Finding Questions

For use during the call with Wawa Productions. This is a fact-finding mission — not a negotiation. Tone should remain professional and neutral throughout. Take notes in the answer fields as they respond.

Before the call: Review Alohi's answers first so you know where the gaps are going in.
After the call: Send a written email summarizing everything discussed. That email is your paper trail.

The Contract & Term

Establish the facts of the current agreement before anything else.

"Can you confirm the current contract runs from April 1, 2026 through April 1, 2027?"

Listen for: any hesitation, a different date range, or mention of amendments.

Saved ✓

"What is the process for non-renewal when April 2027 approaches — what do we need to do, and by what date?"

Listen for: the written notice requirement and exact deadline. Get the specific date confirmed.

The Money

Understand exactly how every dollar flows and who takes what.

"Walk us through exactly how a brand deal payment flows — who receives it first, how is it split, and how long before Alohi sees her portion?"

Listen for: whether the agency holds funds, how long they take, what "1.5% administrative fee" covers.

Saved ✓

"The contract allocates 10% to a Talent Manager. Who specifically is that person, and what do they do for Alohi on a day-to-day basis?"

Listen for: whether this is a real separate person or the same individual. If they can't name them clearly, that's a red flag.

Saved ✓

"Does your commission apply to Alohi's TikTok LIVE income — fan gifts and subscriptions — or only to brand campaigns you negotiate?"

Listen for: any claim on direct platform monetization. Direct fan income should not be subject to agency commission.

Saved ✓

"If Alohi launches her own merchandise — products she designs, funds, and sells independently — does the agency claim any commission on those sales?"

Listen for: any claim on self-created products. Merch she owns and sells directly should be fully hers.

Saved ✓

"Is the $250 annual agency fee current? Has it been charged, and where does that money go?"

Simple factual question. Just confirm it's been charged and acknowledged.

The 65-Hour Streaming Requirement

This requirement does not appear anywhere in the signed contract. That is the starting point for this conversation.

"The 65-hour monthly streaming requirement doesn't appear in the signed contract. Can you show us the written document or amendment that establishes this obligation?"

Do not accept a verbal explanation. Ask for the written document specifically. If they can't produce it, it is not enforceable.

Saved ✓

"What platform does the 65 hours apply to — TikTok LIVE only, or others? How is it tracked and verified?"

Listen for specifics. Vague answers suggest the requirement wasn't thought through.

Saved ✓

"What happens if she doesn't hit 65 hours in a given month? What is the consequence?"

If there's no written consequence, there's no real obligation. Note exactly what they say.

Saved ✓

Deal Activity

Understand what the agency has actually delivered in the past year.

"Can you give us a summary of brand deals you've negotiated for Alohi in the past 6 months — brand names, deal values if possible, and current status?"

A working agency should be able to answer this immediately and specifically. Vague or general answers are a signal.

Saved ✓

"What does the current active pipeline look like — what deals are being worked on right now?"

Listen for names, stages, and timelines. "We're always working on things" is not an answer.

Saved ✓

"How many outbound pitches are you making on Alohi's behalf each month? Which brands are you actively targeting?"

This separates reactive agencies (waiting for inbound) from proactive ones. Expect a number and category list.

Exclusivity & Appearances

The contract grants the agency very broad exclusivity. Understand how they actually enforce it day to day.

"If a brand reaches out to Alohi directly, what's the process? Does she forward it to you, or does she respond first?"

Listen for whether they expect to control all inbound communication, or just final negotiations.

Saved ✓

"If she wants to attend a convention or make a public appearance, what's your approval process? How long does it take?"

The contract requires agency clearance for all public appearances. Understand what that actually means in practice.

Saved ✓

"Are there categories of activity that fall outside the exclusivity — anything she can pursue independently without agency involvement?"

Listen for explicit carve-outs — especially around fan income, personal content, and self-funded merch.

Saved ✓

Looking Ahead

End the call by understanding their vision. This reveals whether they have a real plan or just a contract.

"What are your specific goals for Alohi between now and the end of the current term in April 2027?"

A real agency has targets. Listen for numbers, brand categories, revenue goals. Generic answers are a red flag.

Saved ✓

"What does a successful year look like from your perspective — how do you measure whether you're delivering for Alohi?"

The answer to this question tells you everything about how they view the relationship.
