Roadmap

The master plan — from content creator to business owner. Phased milestones, honest checkpoints, and what success actually looks like.

Where we are now Alohi is evaluating a major life decision — leaving a $60K/year job by July 17th. This roadmap exists to make sure that decision is backed by a real business, not just momentum.

Phase 1 — Foundation (Now → July 17)

Before anything else, the basics have to be in place. This phase is about getting the business structure right so there's something real to jump into.

  • Review and understand the agency contract in full
  • Determine what the agency actually delivers vs. what it costs
  • Form an LLC or confirm existing legal structure
  • Open a dedicated business bank account
  • Set up a business email address
  • Create a real rate card for brand deals
  • Establish a baseline of monthly income and actual expenses
  • Decide: quit July 17th, or push the date with a clearer target?

Phase 2 — Systems (Months 1–3 Post-Job)

Once she's full-time, the focus shifts to building repeatable revenue systems — not just posting and hoping for deposits.

  • Brand deal pipeline: 25–50 outbound pitches per week
  • Merch: launch first product or limited drop
  • Streaming: optimize the 65-hour commitment for actual revenue
  • Accounting: track every expense as a potential write-off
  • Quarterly tax payments begin (self-employment = ~25–30%)
  • Measure: is monthly revenue on pace to match or exceed $60K/year?

Phase 3 — Growth (Months 4–12)

With systems running, the goal becomes compounding — more recurring revenue, fewer one-off deals, and real business independence.

  • Convert brand deals into retainers and ambassador programs
  • Expand merch into a consistent product line
  • Evaluate convention appearances as a revenue stream
  • Build toward Patreon or membership community
  • Review agency relationship: is it still worth the commission?
  • Year-end: file taxes with full write-offs, assess net profitability

Key Decision Points

  • July 17, 2026 — Potential last day at full-time job. Go/no-go decision.
  • Month 3 — Is revenue consistent enough to sustain? Adjust or press on.
  • Month 6 — Agency contract review: renegotiate, renew, or exit?
  • Year 1 — Full tax year as a business. Real profitability assessment.
