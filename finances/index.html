Finances

The real financial picture — not just what comes in, but what actually stays after expenses, taxes, and costs that may currently be invisible.

The hard truth A creator can look profitable while someone else is quietly absorbing the costs. Costumes, props, equipment, software, convention travel — if those aren't tracked as business expenses, the "profit" number is fiction. This section makes the real number visible.

Income Streams

Every source of revenue, tracked separately so we know what's actually working.

  • TikTok Creator Fund / TikTok LIVE gifts and subscriptions
  • Instagram monetization
  • Brand deal payments (agency-sourced)
  • Brand deal payments (self-sourced)
  • Merch sales (net after platform fees)
  • Convention appearances / paid events
  • Patreon or membership income (future)

Business Expenses

These are real costs of doing business — they reduce taxable income and must be tracked. If someone else is paying them, that is a loan or a gift, not free money.

  • Costumes, props, and cosplay materials
  • Camera, lighting, and production equipment
  • Editing software and subscriptions
  • Convention registration and travel
  • Home office / studio space allocation
  • Hair, makeup, styling for content
  • Shipping and packaging (for merch)
  • Business email, website, and tools
  • Agency commission (deducted from gross)

The Salary Replacement Target

Alohi's current employment income is approximately $60,000/year ($5,000/month gross). To replace that as a self-employed creator, she needs to earn significantly more because:

  • Self-employment tax is ~15.3% on top of income tax
  • No employer-sponsored health insurance
  • No 401(k) match or paid time off
  • Inconsistent monthly income — need a buffer
  • Target gross revenue: $80,000–$90,000/year to net equivalent take-home

Externally Fronted Costs

Costs currently being covered by others must be logged here. This is not about guilt — it's about building an accurate picture of what the business actually costs to run.

  • Equipment purchased on behalf of Alohi
  • Costume and production costs covered externally
  • Any travel, convention, or event costs fronted
  • Software or subscription costs paid by others
