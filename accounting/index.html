Accounting

The paper trail — every dollar in and out, documented. This is what gets handed to a CPA at tax time and what protects Alohi in an audit.

Self-employment tax is real As a self-employed creator, Alohi pays both the employee AND employer share of Social Security and Medicare — roughly 15.3% on top of regular income tax. Proper accounting with write-offs is how that number gets smaller.

Invoices

Every brand deal should have an invoice sent before or upon payment. Invoices protect Alohi legally and create a clear paper trail.

  • Invoice template (reusable, with her business name and EIN)
  • Sent invoices by month and brand
  • Payment status tracking (sent, pending, paid)
  • Late payment follow-up log

Expense Receipts

Every business expense needs a receipt. These are the write-offs that reduce taxable income — possibly saving thousands per year. The IRS expects documentation.

  • Costume and prop purchases
  • Camera, lighting, microphone, and production gear
  • Editing software (Adobe, DaVinci, etc.)
  • Convention registration fees
  • Travel: flights, hotels, rideshare (for business purposes)
  • Hair, makeup, and styling for content production
  • Home office: portion of rent/utilities if applicable
  • Business subscriptions and software tools
  • Merch production and shipping costs

Quarterly Tax Estimates

Self-employed creators must pay estimated taxes 4x per year. Missing these results in penalties. Dates to know:

  • Q1: April 15
  • Q2: June 16
  • Q3: September 15
  • Q4: January 15 (following year)
  • Set aside ~25–30% of every payment received

Annual Records

  • 1099-NEC forms (from brands that paid $600+ in a year)
  • TikTok and platform earnings statements
  • Year-end profit and loss summary
  • Prior year tax returns (for reference)
  • CPA contact and annual filing calendar
