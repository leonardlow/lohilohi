Business Banking — How It Works

All content revenue flows to the Amex Blueprint business account. This separation between personal and business money is one of the most important habits you have — don't break it.

  • All platform income (TikTok, YouTube, Patreon, Facebook) → business account
  • All brand deal payments → business account (through agency first, then to you)
  • All business expenses paid from business account — never personal
  • No business credit card yet — consider one for expense tracking clarity
  • Business PayPal in progress — needed for some brand payment methods

LLC — Should You Form One?

Right now, sole proprietorship is fine for your income level. An LLC makes more sense when:

  • Annual income consistently exceeds $50,000+ from the business
  • You're signing larger brand contracts with liability exposure
  • You have significant physical assets (studio, equipment) worth protecting
  • You want to appear more established to enterprise-level brands

When the time comes: Washington State LLC filing fee is $200. Revisit in Phase 2 once income is consistent.

⚠ Before You Launch — Complete This Checklist First
  • Logo finalized and ready for print
  • Written confirmation from Warens that merch is outside the exclusivity
  • Margin analysis done — target 40–60% margin minimum
  • Shopify store set up and tested with a test order
  • Printify product quality verified before going live
  • Launch content planned — tie it to existing audience and a moment (cosplay reveal, convention, viral post)

Margin Reference

Run this calculation on every product before you set a price:

  • Product cost (Printify printing + materials)
  • Shopify transaction fee (~2.9% + $0.30)
  • Shipping to customer (or offer free shipping and build it in)
  • Retail price minus all of the above = your actual margin
  • Target: 40–60% margin minimum — below 40% and it's not worth the effort

First Product Ideas

  • T-shirts or hoodies — Alohi's first instinct, correct for building a fanbase
  • Hats — lower cost, strong margins, wearable daily
  • Tie the first drop to a viral cosplay moment — limited edition increases urgency
  • Digital products (guides, presets) — zero production cost, pure margin, launch anytime
  • Prints from popular cosplay shoots — low cost, high perceived value for fans
